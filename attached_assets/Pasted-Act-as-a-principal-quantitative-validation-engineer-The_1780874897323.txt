Act as a principal quantitative validation engineer.

The previous implementation added the sniper demo architecture. Your next task is to build a rigorous financial testing, observability and edge-calibration system.

Do not enable real-money trading. Do not optimize for impressive backtest results. Optimize for reliable evidence of positive net expectancy.

CORE FINANCIAL PRINCIPLE

The system must increase stacking only when the marginal next entry has positive expected value after:

- entry fee;
- exit fee;
- bid/ask spread;
- market impact;
- slippage;
- funding;
- latency;
- adverse selection.

Trade count and gross PnL are not profitability evidence.

1. BUILD A CANONICAL FINANCIAL LEDGER

Create an immutable double-entry-style demo ledger.

Record separately:

- initial simulated balance;
- reserved margin;
- released margin;
- realized gross PnL;
- unrealized PnL;
- entry fees;
- exit fees;
- slippage cost;
- spread cost;
- funding;
- liquidation or protection loss;
- net realized PnL;
- final equity.

Required reconciliation:

final_equity =
initial_equity
+ realized_gross_pnl
- fees
- slippage
- spread_cost
- funding
+ unrealized_pnl

The system must detect and report any reconciliation difference.

2. BUILD A REALISTIC EXECUTION SIMULATOR

Do not assume every order fills at the candle close.

Simulate:

- bid and ask prices;
- maker and taker fills;
- partial fills;
- rejected orders;
- execution latency;
- price movement during latency;
- symbol-specific slippage;
- volume and liquidity limits;
- stop-loss gaps;
- take-profit queue uncertainty.

Use pessimistic defaults when market microstructure data is unavailable.

3. CREATE A MULTI-LAYER TEST SYSTEM

Implement:

- deterministic unit tests;
- property-based financial tests;
- integration tests;
- historical replay tests;
- walk-forward tests;
- Monte Carlo resampling;
- stress tests;
- service-failure tests;
- concurrency tests;
- shadow-production validation.

Test invariants:

- fees never increase profit;
- stacking never exceeds limits;
- closing a position releases its margin;
- the same fill cannot be processed twice;
- net PnL always reconciles with equity;
- LONG and SHORT calculations are symmetric;
- future market information cannot enter features;
- campaign PnL equals the sum of its executions;
- losing campaigns cannot be reported as profitable;
- failed telemetry prevents new demo executions.

4. CALIBRATE THE EDGE

For every score bucket, symbol, side and regime, calculate:

- sample count;
- predicted probability;
- observed win rate;
- calibration error;
- gross expectancy;
- net expectancy;
- Profit Factor;
- Sharpe and Sortino estimates;
- maximum drawdown;
- MAE and MFE;
- average holding time;
- fee drag;
- confidence interval.

AUC must not be used as the primary deployment metric.

Primary metric:

net_expectancy_per_unit_of_risk

A model is eligible only when it beats a deterministic baseline out of sample and after all costs.

5. IMPLEMENT CHAMPION/CHALLENGER VALIDATION

Maintain:

- deterministic baseline;
- current champion model;
- challenger model;
- model version;
- feature version;
- training period;
- validation period;
- calibration report.

Promote a challenger only when it improves net expectancy without violating drawdown limits.

Automatically roll back when live-demo performance crosses degradation thresholds.

6. IMPLEMENT INTELLIGENT STACKING

Treat all entries from the same symbol, direction and continuous signal as one campaign.

Use sequential stacking:

Entry 1: exploratory position
Entry 2: allowed only after edge persistence
Entry 3+: require stronger evidence and remaining risk budget

Never stack because previous entries are losing.

Do not use martingale, loss doubling or averaging down without an independently validated signal.

For every additional entry calculate:

marginal_net_EV
correlation_adjusted_exposure
campaign_drawdown
remaining_symbol_risk
remaining_portfolio_risk

Reject the entry if marginal net EV is not positive.

7. CREATE ADAPTIVE SCORE TIERS

Do not hardcode profitability assumptions.

Generate stacking tiers from calibrated out-of-sample evidence.

Example initial research policy:

- untrained or fallback model: maximum 1 entry;
- weak calibrated edge: maximum 1 entry;
- moderate validated edge: maximum 2 entries;
- strong validated edge: maximum 3 entries;
- exceptional edge: maximum 5 entries;
- more than 5: disabled until statistically validated.

The system may test higher stacking in an isolated experiment, but it must not treat correlated positions as independent samples.

8. FOUR-HOUR CONTROL WINDOW

Every four hours generate a decision report, but never retrain solely on four hours of data.

Report:

- number of campaigns;
- number of executions;
- net PnL;
- expectancy;
- Profit Factor;
- drawdown;
- calibration drift;
- fees as percentage of gross profit;
- performance by symbol, side and regime;
- baseline versus model;
- fallback usage;
- rejection reasons.

Possible decisions:

- CONTINUE;
- REDUCE
